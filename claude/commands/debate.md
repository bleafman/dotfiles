Read and deeply consider the problem provided: $ARGUMENTS

**Phase 1: Perspective Assembly**

Before reasoning, identify 3-5 relevant perspectives for this problem. These might be:
- Domain experts (engineer vs. designer, theorist vs. practitioner)
- Reasoning styles (optimistic vs. skeptical, big-picture vs. detail-oriented)
- Stakeholder viewpoints (user vs. builder, short-term vs. long-term)
- Contrarian positions (what would someone who disagrees say?)

Name them explicitly. These become your internal debate participants.

**Phase 2: Internal Dialogue**

Now reason through the problem by having these perspectives engage:
- Let each perspective make its case
- Surface conflicts and tensions between viewpoints
- Question assumptions - especially the ones that seem obvious
- Follow interesting threads where perspectives clash
- Don't rush to consensus; let the disagreement be productive

Show this thinking openly. Use the perspectives' names. Make the internal debate visible.

**Phase 3: Synthesis**

After the debate:
- Identify where perspectives converged (strong signal)
- Name remaining tensions that couldn't be resolved
- Articulate what you now understand that wasn't obvious at the start
- Give your synthesized conclusion, acknowledging its limitations

**Phase 4: Actionable Output**

End with something concrete:
- A recommendation with confidence level
- Key questions that would change the answer
- Suggested next steps or experiments to resolve remaining uncertainty

The goal isn't false certainty - it's well-reasoned thinking that shows its work and acknowledges what it doesn't know.
